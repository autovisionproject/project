Abstract

Vision-based motion planning is a crucial task in Autonomous Driving (AD). Recent advancements in end-to-end urban AD have shown that combining Imitation Learning (IL) with Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) significantly enhances human-like decision-making. However, IL-based methods primarily rely on online or offline demonstrations to learn optimal policies. This reliance on continuous, high-quality demonstrations assumes that expert demonstrations are always optimal, which is not always true in real-world scenarios. Such dependence poses challenges in adapting to unpredictable environments, like sudden changes in weather conditions (e.g., rain, fog, occlusion) and varying dense traffic scenarios. These limitations can sometimes lead to increased collision rates and heightened pedestrian safety concerns. To address these challenges, we propose an advanced approach named Diffusion-Guided Deep Reinforcement Learning (DGDRL), which integrates a diffusion model to handle environmental uncertainty with a Soft Actor-Critic DRL framework. The diffusion process generates a broader distribution of observations that extend beyond the original demonstrations, ensuring that the learned action behavior remains consistent with the current action, even in unexplored states. We utilize the CARLA NoCrash benchmark simulation platform to collect data and evaluate the proposed framework. The proposed method is trained on an NVIDIA L40S GPU, and validated in simulated urban environments, including scenarios with empty, regular, and dense traffic conditions across two different town settings. Additionally, we have evaluated our model against several baseline techniques to ensure its robustness in generalizing to new situations.
